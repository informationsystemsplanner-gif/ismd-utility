Information Systems Management Division (ISMD)

A. ISMD Function

Planning, design and development, acquisition and deployment, and monitoring of ICT requirements including related trainings for effective implementation of the information and communications technology resources and administration of NBI Data Center systems and various information systems.

B. Organizational Chart

C. Role and Responsibilities

1. IS Planning Office

a. The Information Systems Planner

Leads the formulation and evaluation of ICT strategic plans, ensuring alignment with national policies and the agency’s mandate.

Responsibilities:

  • Develop and update the agency’s Information Systems Strategic Plan (ISSP).
  • Conduct ICT needs assessments and capability reviews.
  • Monitor and evaluate ICT initiatives for alignment with strategic goals.
  • Coordinate with ISMD and other divisions for policy harmonization and project planning.
  • Provide planning inputs to the Assistant Director, including budgetary and resource recommendations for ICT projects.

2. ISMD Leadership

a. The Chief ISMD

Serves as the head of the ISMD, responsible for directing, managing, and supervising all activities under the division.

Responsibilities:

  • Oversee daily operations and all technical and administrative activities of ISMD.
  • Ensure compliance with national ICT standards, policies, and security protocols.
  • Coordinate with the Assistant Director on division planning, reporting, and policy decisions.
  • Approve project assignments, priorities, and personnel allocations.
  • Lead division-level meetings, performance assessments, and quality control initiatives.

b. The Assistant Chief or Executive Officer

Acts as a senior administrative and operational manager supporting the Chief, ISMD in overseeing the division's functions.The Ex-O ensures smooth coordination of daily operations, policy implementation, and strategic initiatives aligned with the division’s goals. This role serves as a key liaison between the division chief, staff, and external stakeholders, facilitating communication, managing resources, and supporting decision-making processes.

Responsibilities:

  • Assist in the supervision of section heads and technical teams.
  • Oversee the implementation of specific projects or initiatives.
  • Prepare internal reports, work schedules, and documentation as needed by the Chief.
  • Coordinate inter-section communications and resource distribution.
  • Facilitate troubleshooting of cross-cutting operational issues.
  • Designated to sign official documents and correspondences, and to represent the Chief in meetings, workshops, and public forums as needed.

3. ISMD Operational Sections

a. Administrative Support Staff

Provides administrative, clerical, and logistical support to ensure smooth day-to-day operations.

Responsibilities:

  • Maintain records, files, and documentation for ISMD operations.
  • Handle internal communications, scheduling, and coordination of meetings.
  • Facilitate procurement requests, supply inventory, and logistics.
  • Assist with HR-related tasks such as attendance tracking and onboarding of new personnel.
  • Support compliance with administrative requirements (e.g., HRMS, Procurement, Budget, Accounting, Information Division, COA, etc.).
  • E-mail and website administration, including posting and updating of important information.

b. Systems Development and Administration Section

Designs, develops, maintains, and upgrades in-house systems and applications to support agency operations.

Responsibilities:

  • Perform systems analysis, design, coding, and testing of software solutions.
  • Maintain and enhance existing applications based on user feedback and evolving requirements including email and website application.
  • Analyze user and business requirements to design and develop effective software solutions.
  • Write, test, debug, and maintain code for various applications using modern programming languages.
  • Develop backend systems, APIs, and database integrations to support business processes and system interoperability.
  • Document process flows, system workflows and business process flowcharts for system development purposes.
  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams including other technical personnel, project managers, and end-users to ensure system functionality meets organizational needs.
  • Manage software development lifecycle activities with the implementation of version control, documentation, and secure coding practices.
  • Perform regular system monitoring, optimization, and updates to ensure stability and security.
  • Participate in code reviews and enforce coding standards and best practices in terms of efficiency, security and delivery or service.
  • Automate repetitive tasks through scripting and other automation tools.
  • Assist in technical support and troubleshooting for system-related issues.
  • Stay current with emerging technologies and recommend improvements or new tools.
  • Mentor upcoming developers and contribute to continuous team learning and development.
  • Collaborate with users for application rollout, training, and support.
  • Create and maintain technical documentation, user manuals, and training materials.

b.1. Computer Emergency Response Team

The CERT is a team that handles computer security incidents, especially those reported by the National Computer Emergency Response Team (NCERT or CERT-PH). They help protect the Bureau’s systems, respond to cyber threats, and assist in recovering from attacks. The team also shares important cybersecurity information and works with external agencies to improve overall security.

Responsibilities:

  • Primary point of contact of the NCERT or CERT-PH for incident response, threat intelligence, security information sharing, guidance and support.
  • Coordinate with the concerned technical personnel, divisions and other bodies to promptly resolve any security incidents such as data breaches, malware attacks, and denial-of-service attacks.
  • Gather and analyze information about new and emerging cyber threats to stay ahead of potential attacks and provide guidance on how to prevent, detect, and respond to security incidents.
  • Facilitate any vulnerability assessment and penetration testing activities in coordination with involved technical personnel, NCERT and third party agencies.
  • Share official security alerts, warnings, and best practices from NCERT and other authoritative agencies with end-users and other stakeholders to raise awareness and improve overall security.
  • Participate in public awareness campaigns to educate users about cybersecurity risks and best practices.
  • Engage in research to improve security systems and develop new tools and techniques for protecting against cyber threats in coordination with NCERT and other relevant authoritative agencies.

c. Database Management and Administration Section

Manages the lifecycle, performance, and security of databases and major application systems.

Responsibilities:

  • Design and maintain database schemas and data models.
  • Perform routine database administration tasks (e.g., backup, recovery, tuning).
  • Manage application hosting environments and deployment pipelines.
  • Monitor system performance and troubleshoot issues.
  • Ensure data integrity, security, and availability in compliance with regulations.

c.1. Project Management Team

The Project Management Team leads and oversees the planning, execution, monitoring, and successful completion of ISMD projects of any scale. It ensures that all projects are delivered on time, within scope and budget, and in alignment with the organization's strategic objectives. The team coordinates resources, manages risks, and maintains clear communication with stakeholders to drive the delivery of technology solutions that meet business needs.

Responsibilities:

  • Lead the full project lifecycle for information systems initiatives, including planning, scheduling, budgeting, resource allocation, risk management, and stakeholder communication.
  • Collaborate with cross-functional teams including administrative, technical, developers, end-users, vendors, and external partners to ensure project objectives align with organizational goals.
  • Facilitate communication and collaboration among project team members and stakeholders to ensure transparency and alignment.
  • Define project scope, goals, and deliverables that support business objectives in collaboration with senior management and stakeholders.
  • Develop detailed documents that constitute the project folders which include plans, timelines, and milestones; monitor progress and adjust plans as necessary to meet deadlines.
  • Ensure compliance with organizational policies, industry standards, and regulatory requirements.
  • Prepare and present regular project status reports, dashboards, and documentation for senior leadership.
  • Lead change management efforts related to information systems implementations and upgrades.
  • Drive continuous improvement initiatives to enhance project management processes and methodologies within the division.

d. Technical Support and User Assistance Section

Provides front-line IT support services, user assistance, and basic training to personnel.

Responsibilities:

  • Operate the help desk and respond to user queries and incidents.
  • Diagnose and resolve hardware, software, and network issues.
  • Set up and maintain user accounts, workstations, and peripheral devices.
  • Provide orientation and training for users on basic ICT tools and services.
  • Escalate complex issues to the appropriate technical units for resolution.
